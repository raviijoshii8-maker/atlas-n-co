raw denim & selvedge trail
📍 denim quarter · molochnyi lane 4
selvedge district · dark edition
👖the loom room

what to scout

  • selvedge ID: red, white or custom thread indicates shuttle loom fabric.
  • unsanforized denim: expect 7-10% shrinkage — hot soak for custom fit.
  • hidden rivets, raised belt loops & lined back pockets = hallmark of quality.
  • price reference: $185–495 (japanese mills / small batch).
🔍 insider note: ask for "once-washed" version to skip shrink guess — many artisans in the quarter offer free chainstitch hemming.
flight jackets · aviator archive
📍 aerodrome market · upper gallery row 12

hunting the perfect jacket

  • goatskin vs horsehide: goatskin soft & lightweight, horsehide develops rich patina.
  • original Talon, Crown or Conmar zippers = pre-80s authenticity.
  • check lining: alpaca or wool blends for true vintage insulation.
  • price range: $190–650 depending on contract stamps & condition.
✈️ patina speaks — natural grain & undertones add story. avoid jackets with cracked finishes or dry rot.
silk route · foulard archive
📍 šovkovy passage · textile court 7

scarf & blouse treasures

  • hand-rolled hems — hallmark of pre-1990s luxury silk.
  • designer signatures: hermes, yves saint laurent, dior originals retain value.
  • the burn test (ask politely): real silk smells like burnt hair, synthetic melts.
  • price: $45–380 for museum-quality pieces or rare prints.
tailoring atelier · made-to-measure
📍 via dei sarti · liberty street 22

commission guide

  • full canvas construction vs fused — canvas molds to body over time.
  • mill fabrics: vitale barberis, fox brothers, or local tweeds (280–450g).
  • request surgeon cuffs, horn buttons & pick stitching for bespoke finish.
  • investment: $750–1650 for custom suits & overcoats.
🧵 two fitting rule: basted fitting for structure + final fitting for silhouette perfection.
cashmere lodge · vintage knits
📍 highland bazaar · wool arcade west

knitwear scouting

  • 2-ply vs 4-ply: higher ply = warmer and more durable.
  • check cuffs & collar for thinning — minor pilling can be removed with cashmere comb.
  • labels: "100% cashmere", preferably scottish or italian origins.
  • price: $65–210 for vintage ballantyne, pringle, or johnston.
vegetable tanned · leather studio
📍 cuoio bottega · tannery row 9

quality markers

  • full-grain leather: retains natural texture and ages beautifully.
  • saddle stitching (hand-stitched) > machine stitching for durability.
  • edges: burnished and waxed, never plastic-coated.
  • price: belts $95–170, bags $350–950 (custom hardware).
🔪 ask about tannery origin: italian (walpier, tempesti) or french — each offers unique patina evolution.
linen shirt · weaving guild
📍 linen passage · belgian quarter

shopping signals

  • 100% european flax (irish or normandy) — medium weight 160–200gsm.
  • reinforced gussets at underarm, mother-of-pearl or corozo buttons.
  • garment washed for softness without losing structure.
  • price: $110–260 made-to-order or ready-to-wear capsule.
workwear archive · chore coats
📍 railway vintage · old depot market

utility & durability

  • duck cloth or heavy canvas 12–15oz, corduroy collar, triple-stitched seams.
  • union labels, talon zippers, or donut buttons = authentic vintage (pre-70s).
  • provenance: carhartt, lee, pointer brand, or french chore coats.
  • price: $80–280 depending on patina and era.
🪡 vintage workwear runs large — measure pit-to-pit and shoulder width for accurate sizing.
